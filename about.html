About the Movement

Structured Optimism

We believe in liberation—not just from economic insecurity, but toward human flourishing in a world where work is optional.

Our Mission

Post-Labor Economics (PLE) is building the intellectual and policy frameworks for a world where human dignity and economic security are no longer dependent on selling your time. We're not preparing for job loss—we're preparing for liberation.

As automation, artificial intelligence, and new economic models reshape the relationship between labor and value, we need new frameworks that center human flourishing over productivity metrics.

Our Philosophy: Structured Optimism

We call our approach "Structured Optimism"—the belief that a better future is possible, combined with the rigor and discipline to actually build it. This means:

  • Evidence-based thinking: Grounding our proposals in research and data
  • Pragmatic idealism: Balancing ambitious vision with practical implementation
  • Open collaboration: Building in public with transparent governance
  • Inclusive participation: Centering diverse voices in all decisions

What We Do

Research & Analysis

We study UBI pilots, automation trends, alternative economic models, and policy experiments worldwide.

Community Building

We connect researchers, advocates, policymakers, and citizens who share our vision of prosperity beyond work.

Policy Development

We develop concrete policy proposals through collaborative governance and rigorous analysis.

Public Education

We create accessible content that helps people understand and engage with post-labor economics.

The L/0 Symbol

Our symbol represents the threshold between Labor and Zero—the transition point where economic security becomes independent of traditional employment. The forward slash represents passage, movement, and transformation. It's not about the end of work, but the beginning of choice.
