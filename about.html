This Isn't New

Thinkers have imagined this for centuries

The idea that humans might one day be freed from compulsory labor isn't science fiction. It's a thread that runs through economic thought from its foundations.

1797
Thomas Paine ‚Äî "Agrarian Justice"
Proposed a universal endowment for every citizen, funded by a land tax. One of the earliest UBI-like proposals.
1930
Keynes ‚Äî "Economic Possibilities for our Grandchildren"
Predicted that by 2030, humanity would work 15-hour weeks. The technology arrived; the economic structures didn't.
1964
The Triple Revolution Memo
Scientists and economists warned President Johnson that cybernation would permanently alter the labor market. They were early, but not wrong.
2020s
The AI Inflection
Large language models and generative AI demonstrate that cognitive labor ‚Äî the last "safe" category ‚Äî is now automatable. The conversation shifts from "if" to "when" and "how."

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Are you anti-work?

+
No. We're pro-choice. We believe people should be free to work on what matters to them ‚Äî not forced into labor for survival. The goal is expanding human agency, not eliminating productivity.

Isn't this just UBI advocacy?

+
UBI is one tool we study, but post-labor economics is broader. We explore robot taxes, universal basic services, cooperative ownership, shortened work weeks, public investment funds, and entirely new economic models. The question isn't "what single policy?" but "what system redesign?"

How can I contribute?

+
Create an account and start anywhere: write articles, comment on discussions, vote on proposals, join projects, or simply read and share. Every perspective strengthens the movement.

Is this a political movement?

+
We're nonpartisan. The transition away from labor-dependent economics affects everyone regardless of political orientation. Our governance is democratic, our research is evidence-based, and all perspectives are welcome.

How is the platform governed?

+
Transparently. Anyone can submit proposals. Members vote on changes. Architecture decisions are documented publicly. The platform is open source, and all governance activity is visible in the activity feed.
